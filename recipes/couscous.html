Couscous

Description

Couscous is a cherished staple of Moroccan cuisine, often considered the heart of family meals and celebrations. Made from steamed semolina grains, it serves as a versatile base for a variety of toppings. Traditional Moroccan couscous is typically paired with a medley of vegetables, tender meats like lamb or chicken, and aromatic spices such as cinnamon, ginger, and turmeric. The dish is often enjoyed on Fridays, symbolizing community and togetherness. Its light, fluffy texture and ability to absorb the rich flavors of the broth make couscous a comforting and flavorful centerpiece of Moroccan culinary traditions.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups couscous
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 cup chickpeas, cooked
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • 1/4 cup slivered almonds, toasted
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro for garnish

Steps to Make Moroccan Couscous

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and saut√© until translucent.
  2. Add the carrots, zucchini, ground cinnamon, cumin, turmeric, salt, and pepper. Cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Pour in the vegetable or chicken broth and bring it to a boil.
  4. Stir in the couscous, cover the pot, and remove it from heat. Let it sit for about 5 minutes or until the couscous absorbs the broth.
  5. Fluff the couscous with a fork and gently mix in the chickpeas, raisins, and toasted almonds.
  6. Transfer the couscous to a serving platter and garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro.
  7. Serve warm and enjoy this delicious Moroccan dish!
